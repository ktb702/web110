HTML Forms

Forms are probably the most important aspects of HTML. It is one of the best ways to get input from prospective clients and establish a relationship with them. Your prospective clients can use your forms to contact you to ask questions, give feedback, request additional information, or order products/services. But forms can be a hindrance if not used correctly. If the questions on your forms are inappropriate, confusing, or you are asking too many questions it can put users off and tarnish your brand. For example, “Expedia learned they were losing $12 million a year in profits because of one extraneous form field box alone”¹ according to Kristen Baker of Hubspot.

The goal of any website that uses forms should be to make them as easy to use and user friendly as possible, keeping accessibility in mind also. According to Adriana Neagu of formotus, “fewer than two percent of web page viewers opt-in to forms. Most visitors walk away”². Here is a list of what to keep in mind as we design forms:

  • Ask only what is necessary. Keep your form as simple and short as possible.
  • Group related fields together (such as personal / mailing / account information).
  • List fields in a logical sequence.
  • Use a single column layout.
  • Use input constraints and clear error messages.
  • Auto fill info for the user whenever possible.
  • Use clear labels and placeholders to let the user know exactly what information / format you are looking for.
  • Use real-time inline validation.
  • Provide feedback to users.
  • Explain why you are asking for sensitive information.
  • Be aware of color contrast.

Users can be hesitant to fill out forms, especially if they are long and confusing. It’s important to design forms with this in mind and keep them as user friendly as possible.
